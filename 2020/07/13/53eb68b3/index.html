Prose_Blogs of Sociologists

Posted on 2020-07-13 Edited on 2023-06-04 In History of Science Waline: Word count in article: 4.3k Reading time ≈ 7 mins.

Actually, I wish to collect the sociologists’ blog sites have been long. This essay collects blogs that are still updated now, I hope this collection is helpful for anyone who reading this essay now. This eassy will be updated regularly.
The first updated was on April 18, 2018, and the second updated was on July 13, 2020.

1. Personal Blogs

(1) USA

Yuqiong Li Brief introduction: Master student at New York University Center of Data Science. A graduate student in sociology once, working on social movements and cultural sociology. I feel deep regret for she deleted many articles she wrote in her early years, including reflection articles from sociological perspective, and some novels called “Bo Fulin’s love story (薄扶林爱情故事)”.

眼見Yuqiong Li的新博文，不知該評說些什麼。其因雨傘運動的觸動，從統計轉入社會系，又因現實壓迫轉至金融，滋味難盡，亦是我失去了一個可以先導的前輩。日後我或也轉入市场，卻也是沒有說法的事情了，人世畢竟各種各樣的限制太多，而我始終搖擺。2016.11.9 (I don’t know what to say when I see Yuqiong Li’s new blog post. Touched by the umbrella movement, she transferred from the Department of statistics to the Department of sociology, and then transferred to finance because of the reality. I’ve lost an academic predecessor. In the future, I may also enter the market to apply for a job, which is also unpredictable. After all, there are too many restrictions in the world, and I always vacillate.)

Dadao Hou Brief introduction: I used to read all his articles all night. He is a sexual minority. He majored in statistics at Peking University, then he majored in demography in Hong Kong, and then went to Texas A & M University to study sociology. I really like his words. However, it is difficult to find out where he is and what he is doing now through the Internet.
施永仁 Brief introduction: Associate Professor of Sociology at University of Iowa. He focuses on social network analysis.
kieran healy Brief introduction: Associate Professor of Sociology at Duke University.

(2) England

张悦悦/Joy Brief introduction: Senior Lecturer／Associate Professor in Sociology at University of Kent.
Mark Carrigan Brief introduction: A researcher in the Culture Politics and Global Justice cluster in the Faculty of Education at the University of Cambridge. He tends to describe himself as a digital sociologist.
Elder Brief introduction: A Reader in the Department of Social Sciences at Loughborough University.

(3) Australia

This Sociological Life, Deborah Lupton Brief introduction: Centenary Research Professor in the Faculty of Arts & Design, University of Canberra.

(4) Taiwan

曾柏文 Brief introduction: Albert Tzeng was born in Taipei and moved seven times before he was seven years old. He spent his childhood in Taichung from elementary school to high school. Tzeng transferred from the Department of Chemistry to the Department of Psychology at Taiwan University. He then went to LSE to study sociology. I am very interested in his doctoral thesis ‘Framing Sociology in Taiwan, Hong Kong and Singapore’ at Warwick University.
張育誠 Brief introduction: Yucheng is a master student majoring in sociology at Taiwan University. He is my classmate in the course of social statistics taught by Professor Gaohua Ye in 2016.

(5) Mainland

王潇宇 Brief introduction: He has a bachelor’s degree in sociology from Yunnan Normal University and a master’s degree in ethnic history from Yunnan University. He seems to be working in Yunnan FROM OUR EYES Rural Documentary and Culture Research Centre, His recent situation can be seen on this website.
An unnamed sociologist Brief introduction: A bachelor of arts in history, but he’s now turn to sociology.

2. My friends who majoring in sociology

Yukun Ma Brief introduction: My best friend in graduate school, Yukun used to major in engineering at Nanjing University, but he’s now pursuing for a PhD in sociology.
Jin Zhu Brief introduction: My undergraduate roommate, he’s now majoring in sociology at Xi’an Jiaotong University.
Cheng Lin Brief introduction: Graduate student in computational social sciences, Linköping University, Sweden.

3. Public Blogs

But some blogs are not written by individuals. These blogs are co authored by a group of sociologists, as shown below.

(1) USA

Everyday sociology Brief introduction: Karen Sternheimer teaches in the sociology department at the University of Southern California; Sally Raskoff has been employed as a sociologist since the mid 1980s and is currently the Chair of Sociology and Ethnic Studies at Los Angeles Valley College; Peter Kaufman; Teresa Irene Gonzales…… etc.
The Society Pages Brief introduction: The Society Pages (TSP) is an open-access social science project headquartered in the Department of Sociology at the University of Minnesota and supported by individual donors.

(2) England

Sociological imagination Brief introduction: Mark Carrigan, Milena Kremakova, Sadia Habib.

(3) Taiwan

巷仔口社會學 Brief introduction: A blog which written by the group of Taiwan sociologists.
菜市場政治學 Brief introduction: A blog which written by the group of Taiwan politicians.

(4) Mainland

社会学吧 Brief introduction: Held by Nanjing University.
